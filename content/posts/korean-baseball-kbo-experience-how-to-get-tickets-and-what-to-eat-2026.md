## Understanding the KBO League: Korea's Passion for Baseball

The Korea Baseball Organization (KBO) League is the top professional baseball league in South Korea, renowned for its high-energy games and exceptionally passionate fan base. Established in 1982, the KBO has grown into a cornerstone of Korean sports and entertainment. The league typically features 10 teams, each with a fiercely loyal following, competing in a regular season from late March/early April to October, followed by an exciting playoff series culminating in the Korean Series championship.

### KBO Teams and Their Home Stadiums

Each KBO team boasts a unique identity, mascot, and a dedicated home stadium that becomes a fortress of fan fervor during game days. Understanding the teams and their locations can help you choose which game to attend, especially if you have a preference for a particular city or atmosphere.

*   **Doosan Bears / LG Twins:** Both share Jamsil Baseball Stadium in Seoul, making it one of the most accessible venues for visitors.
*   **SSG Landers:** Based in Incheon, playing at Incheon SSG Landers Field.
*   **Kiwoom Heroes:** Play at Gocheok Sky Dome in Seoul, Korea's only domed baseball stadium, perfect for any weather.
*   **NC Dinos:** Hailing from Changwon, with their home at NC Park Masan.
*   **Samsung Lions:** Based in Daegu, playing at Daegu Samsung Lions Park.
*   **Lotte Giants:** The pride of Busan, playing at Sajik Baseball Stadium, famous for its incredibly enthusiastic crowds.
*   **KT Wiz:** Located in Suwon, their home is Suwon KT Wiz Park.
*   **Hanwha Eagles:** From Daejeon, playing at Daejeon Hanwha Life Eagles Park.
*   **Kia Tigers:** A storied franchise from Gwangju, their home is Gwangju-Kia Champions Field.

### The KBO Season at a Glance

The KBO regular season is long and packed with action. For the 2026 season, expect the schedule to run from late March/early April through late September or early October. Each team plays 144 games. Weekday games typically start in the evening (around 6:30 PM), while weekend and holiday games often have afternoon starts (around 2:00 PM or 5:00 PM). It's crucial to check the official KBO schedule or team websites for exact game times and dates closer to your travel period in 2026. Keep an eye out for special themed nights or promotional events for an even richer experience.

## How to Get KBO Tickets for 2026

Securing your tickets is the first step to enjoying the KBO magic. While it might seem challenging at first glance, several methods cater to international visitors.

### Online Ticketing Platforms

The primary online platform for KBO tickets is **Interpark Ticket**.

*   **Interpark Ticket (Global Website):** This is your best bet. Interpark often provides a global English version of their site, making the purchasing process much smoother for non-Korean speakers. You'll typically need to create an account, select your desired game and seats, and pay with an international credit card. Tickets are usually released a week or two in advance for most games, but popular matchups or weekend games can sell out quickly.
    *   **Pro Tip:** Check Interpark Ticket's website well in advance of the 2026 season for updated information on ticket release dates and any changes to their global service.

*   **Team-Specific Websites:** Some teams might offer direct ticket sales through their official websites. While these are often primarily in Korean, a browser's translation feature can sometimes help. However, Interpark Ticket usually aggregates sales for all teams, making it the most convenient single point of access.

### Buying Tickets at the Stadium

While online purchase is recommended for convenience and to avoid disappointment, walk-up tickets might be available for less popular weekday games or teams.

*   **Ticket Booths:** Most stadiums have ticket booths that open a few hours before game time.
*   **Language Barrier:** Be prepared for a potential language barrier if buying in person. Having the game date, teams playing, and preferred seating section written down can be very helpful.
*   **Payment:** Credit cards are widely accepted, but it's always good to have some Korean Won cash as a backup.
*   **Warning:** Do not rely on buying tickets at the stadium for popular weekend games, rivalry matchups (like Doosan vs. LG at Jamsil), or games involving the Lotte Giants in Busan, as these often sell out completely online.
