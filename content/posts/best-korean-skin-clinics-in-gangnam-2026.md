## Essential Tips for International Patients

Embarking on a medical tourism journey requires careful planning. Here are some indispensable tips for a smooth and successful experience.

### Booking & Consultations
*   **Book in Advance:** Popular clinics and doctors can be fully booked weeks or even months ahead, especially during peak travel seasons.
*   **Online Consultations:** Many clinics offer preliminary online consultations via video call or email. This is a great way to discuss your concerns, get a rough estimate, and assess the clinic's English communication capabilities before you even arrive.
*   **Be Specific:** Clearly articulate your skin concerns, desired outcomes, and any previous treatments or allergies during the consultation. Bring photos if you have specific "before" images or reference photos of desired results.

### Language Support & Translation Services
*   **Dedicated Coordinators:** Most reputable Gangnam clinics for international patients have dedicated English-speaking (and often Chinese, Japanese, or Russian) coordinators. They will guide you through the entire process, from consultation to post-care.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1: Do I need a visa to get treatments in Korea?**
A1: Visa requirements vary by nationality. Many countries have visa-free entry for short stays, but some may require a K-ETA (Korea Electronic Travel Authorization) or a full visa. Always check the official Korean embassy or consulate website in your country well in advance of your travel dates.

**Q2: How long should I plan to stay in Korea for skin treatments?**
A2: For single, non-invasive treatments, 3-5 days might suffice, allowing for consultation, treatment, and initial recovery. For multi-session treatments (like lasers for pigmentation) or more invasive procedures (e.g., extensive thread lifts), 1-2 weeks is advisable to allow for multiple sessions and adequate downtime for swelling or bruising to subside. Always discuss the recommended recovery time with your doctor.

**Q3: Is it safe to get multiple treatments at once?**
A3: This depends entirely on the type of treatments, their invasiveness, and your individual skin condition. A reputable dermatologist will assess your suitability for combined treatments and prioritize safety. Generally, combining complementary, non-invasive treatments (e.g., a laser toning with a skin booster) might be fine, but multiple invasive procedures at once are often not recommended. Always follow your doctor's professional advice.

**Q4: Can I get a refund if I'm not satisfied with the results?**
A4: Policies vary significantly between clinics. Most clinics will not offer a full refund purely based on dissatisfaction with aesthetic results, as outcomes can be subjective and influenced by individual healing. However, if there was a clear medical error or a complication directly caused by the treatment, clinics are generally responsible for corrective measures. Always clarify the clinic's refund and revision policy before starting any treatment.

**Q5: What are the typical payment methods accepted at Korean skin clinics?**
A5: Most Korean skin clinics accept major international credit cards (Visa, MasterCard, American Express). Cash payments in Korean Won (?? are also widely accepted. Some clinics may offer installment plans for very expensive treatments, but this is less common for international patients. Always confirm accepted payment methods with your chosen clinic beforehand.
