## Paragliding in Korea: Soaring over the peninsula
Paragliding has exploded in popularity, driven partly by its appearance in K-Dramas like *Crash Landing on You*. The experience is generally a tandem flight, meaning you are strapped to a certified pilot who handles the technical launch and landing, leaving you to enjoy the view and take photos.

### The best spots for paragliding
There are two main hubs for paragliding near the capital region: Danyang and Yangpyeong.

**1. Danyang (Para Village)**
Danyang is the undisputed capital of Korean paragliding. Located in North Chungcheong Province, about 2.5 hours from Seoul by bus, it offers spectacular views of the Namhan River winding around the Sobaeksan Mountains. The launch sites here are approximately 600 meters above sea level.

*   **Flight Duration:** 10 to 15 minutes (weather dependent).

**2. Yangpyeong**
If you are pressed for time, Yangpyeong is closer to Seoul (about 60-90 minutes). The famous Yumyeongsan Mountain launch site (860 meters) is higher than Danyang, offering longer flight potential, though the river views are less dramatic than Danyang's oxbow turns.

### What to expect on flight day
The process is streamlined for efficiency. You do not need prior training.
1.  **Arrival:** You will be picked up from the local station or drive to the landing zone office.
2.  **Gear Up:** Wear a flight suit over your clothes (provided).
3.  **Transport:** A truck takes you up a steep, winding road to the summit.
4.  **Launch:** You run forward with the pilot; do not jump or sit until you are airborne.

<InfoBox type="warning" title="Motion Sickness Warning">
Paragliding involves spirals and rapid descents if you ask for the "dynamic" experience. If you are prone to motion sickness, take medication 30 minutes before the flight and ask your pilot for a "gentle" ride.
</InfoBox>

## Whitewater rafting: Conquering Korean rapids
Summer in Korea (June to August) is hot and humid, making water sports essential. Korean rafting is community-focused, often involving team games, splashing wars, and a stop for Korean snacks midway. While you won't find the Class 5 death-defying rapids of the Zambezi, Korea offers solid Class 2 to Class 3+ rapids that are thrilling yet safe for beginners.

### Top rafting locations

**1. Inje (Naerincheon River)**
Inje is the premier rafting destination, located in Gangwon Province. The Naerincheon River flows north, a rarity in Korea, and features the fastest currents.
*   **Course Length:** approx. 6km to 20km options.
*   **Duration:** 2 to 3 hours.
*   **Rapid Class:** 3 (up to 4 after heavy rain).
*   **Best Time:** July to mid-August.

**2. Yeongwol (Donggang River)**
The Donggang River offers a more scenic, leisurely experience. It flows through limestone cliffs and is ecologically protected. This is better for families or those who want to enjoy the scenery more than the adrenaline.
*   **Rapid Class:** 2 (gentle).
*   **Scenery:** 10/10 (Limestone karst landscape).

## Rock climbing and bouldering: Granite paradise
Korea is a mountainous peninsula formed largely of granite, offering friction climbing that is world-renowned. The climbing culture here is intense, with locals decked out in high-end gear.

### Indoor climbing gyms (Bouldering)
If you are visiting in winter or the rainy season, Seoul has a density of climbing gyms that rivals Tokyo. The dominant franchise is **"The Climb,"** with major branches in Yeonnam, Magok, and Gangnam.
*   **Day Pass Cost:** â‚©20,000 â€“ â‚©25,000.
*   **Shoe Rental:** â‚©3,000 â€“ â‚©5,000.
*   **Style:** Modern, competition-style setting with frequent resets.

### Outdoor climbing: Bukhansan National Park
Located on the northern edge of Seoul, Bukhansan is unique because you can take a subway to the base of a national park. The main attraction is **Insu-bong**, a granite dome rising about 200 meters from its base (810m above sea level).

### Seoraksan National Park: Ulsanbawi
For the ultimate adventure, Ulsanbawi in Seoraksan (near Sokcho) offers multi-pitch climbing on a massive granite formation. It requires a 3-hour drive from Seoul. ## Practical logistics for adventurers
### Insurance is non-negotiable
While Korean operators have insurance, it typically covers only basic liability. If you are engaging in "hazardous sports" (which climbing and paragliding often are classified as), standard travel insurance policies may void your coverage. Ensure your policy specifically includes "adventure sports coverage."

### Booking platforms vs. Direct
*   **Klook/Trazy:** Best for English speakers. Prices are slightly higher (5-10%) but customer service handles the language barrier.
*   **Naver Booking:** If you have a Korean friend or can navigate Hangul, booking directly via Naver Maps often yields a 10-20% discount and more time slot options.

## Other adventure activities
Beyond the "big three," Korea is diversifying its adventure portfolio.
*   **Luge:** Tongyeong and Ganghwa Island have massive Luge tracks. The Skyline Luge in Tongyeong offers a 1.5km track with ocean views.
*   **Ziplining:** The Nami Island zipline is famous, but for a more intense experience, the **Hadong Alps Zipline** is the longest in Asia (3.18km) and reaches speeds of 120km/h.
*   **Surfing:** Yangyang (Surfyy Beach) on the east coast is the surfing capital. While waves are generally mellow (best for beginners/longboarders), typhoon season brings serious swells.
