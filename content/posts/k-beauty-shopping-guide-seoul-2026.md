## Neighborhood Shopping Guide: Where to Go

Seoul is massive, covering 605 square kilometers. To optimize your shopping, you should target specific districts based on your preferences. In 2026, the geography of beauty shopping has shifted distinctively toward three main hubs.

### 1. Seongsu-dong: The Indie & Pop-up Hub
Once a shoe-making industrial zone, Seongsu is now the "Brooklyn of Seoul" and the hottest destination for Gen Z travelers. This is where you find aesthetics-driven brands, conceptual pop-up stores, and niche perfumes. The average distance between major pop-up spaces here is only 200 meters, making it highly walkable.

**Why go here?** For brands like Tamburins, Amore Pacific, and countless indie labels that launch exclusive pop-ups here weekly.

### 2. Myeongdong: The Revival of the Classic
After a slump in the early 2020s, Myeongdong has fully recovered by 2026. It remains the best place for "road shop" brands (single-brand stores) like Innisfree, Etude House, and Nature Republic. The district packs over 100 cosmetic stores into a 0.4 square kilometer radius.

**Pros:** High density of stores, multilingual staff (English, Japanese, Chinese), and generous free samples (often 5-10 sachets per purchase).
**Cons:** Can be intensely crowded; prices are sometimes higher than online unless there is a sale.

### 3. Gangnam & Apgujeong: Luxury & Clinics
If your budget allows for high-end skincare (Sulwhasoo, Hera) or dermatology treatments, head south of the river. The "Dosan Park" area in Apgujeong hosts luxury flagship stores that function more like art galleries than retail spaces.
