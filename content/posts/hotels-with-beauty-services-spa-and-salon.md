## Specialized Services: Head Spas and Scalp Clinics

In 2026, the "Korean Head Spa" has become a global viral phenomenon. This 15-step scalp treatment involves deep cleansing, scaling, massage, and conditioning. Many hotels with beauty services spa and salon have added dedicated scalp clinics to their amenities list.

### What to Expect in a Hotel Head Spa

Unlike a standard hair wash, a head spa is a therapeutic procedure.
1.  **Diagnosis:** A microscopic camera analyzes your scalp condition (oily, dry, hair loss).
2.  **Scaling:** A menthol-based scaling agent removes dead skin cells.
3.  **Steam:** A steam helmet opens pores for 10-15 minutes.
4.  **Massage:** Intense acupressure on the neck and shoulders.
5.  **Shampoo & Tonic:** Customized products applied.

### Language and Payment
Most 5-star hotels with beauty services spa and salon have fluent English-speaking staff. However, if you venture to boutique hotels or their partner clinics, translation apps like Papago are essential.

Payment is seamless in hotels; you can charge treatments to your room. This is particularly helpful for tracking spending. Remember that tipping is *not* customary in Korea, even in luxury spas. The price you see includes service charges and tax.

## Best Areas to Stay for Beauty Lovers

If your priority is access to beauty services, choosing the right neighborhood is critical.

### Gangnam (The Beauty Belt)
Staying in Gangnam (near Gangnam Station, Sinnonhyeon, or Apgujeong) places you in the heart of the action. Hotels here are modern and built with the medical tourist in mind.

This area is better for "skincare shopping" and mid-range facials.
*   **Pros:** Walkable to major department stores (Lotte, Shinsegae) and street food.
