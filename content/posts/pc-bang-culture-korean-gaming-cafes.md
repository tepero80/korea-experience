## What is a PC Bang?

A PC Bang is a dedicated gaming center equipped with high-performance computers designed primarily for multiplayer online gaming. Unlike the dusty, utilitarian internet cafÃ©s found in Europe or Southeast Asia, Korean PC Bangs are luxury experiences. As of 2026, there are over 18,000 PC Bangs operating across South Korea, with the highest concentration in the Seoul Metropolitan area.

## The Registration and Payment Process

Navigating a PC Bang as a foreigner can initially seem confusing because the interface is often in Korean. However, the process has become significantly more streamlined with the adoption of automated kiosks (machines) at the entrance of every establishment.

The payment model is prepaid. You purchase a block of time, and once that time expires, the computer automatically logs you out. Most venues operate on a strict "pay first" basis for non-members, though members can sometimes accrue charges to pay upon exit.

## Safety, Etiquette, and The "Sleeping" Question

Is it safe? Absolutely. South Korea is one of the safest countries globally, and PC Bangs are heavily monitored by CCTV. Theft is rare, though standard precautions should always apply.

Regarding noise: While gaming can get heated, shouting or screaming is generally frowned upon in the open areas. However, the mechanical keyboards used (usually Cherry MX Blue switches) create a distinct "clacking" hum that permeates the room. It is a form of white noise that many locals find comforting.

### Can You Sleep at a PC Bang?
Technically, yes, but it is not a hotel. The chairs are comfortable enough to recline and nap, and you will see many locals doing this during the early morning hours (3:00 AM - 6:00 AM). However, there are no showers or private beds. It is a survival option, not a luxury one.

## Important Regulations for Travelers

There is one critical rule that affects travelers, especially younger ones. Under the Juvenile Protection Act, minors (under age 19) are strictly prohibited from entering PC Bangs between 10:00 PM and 9:00 AM.

Police patrols frequently check IDs at 10:00 PM. If you look young, expect the staff to ask for your passport. If you are underage, you will be politely but firmly asked to leave, regardless of how much time is left on your account.
