### 1. The Shilla Seoul The Parkview: A Benchmark of Luxury Dining

When discussing premium all-you-can-eat experiences in Seoul, The Shilla Seoul's The Parkview consistently tops the list. Nestled within one of Seoul's most prestigious hotels, The Parkview offers an unparalleled dining experience that blends exquisite international cuisine with a sophisticated ambiance. It’s more than just a meal; it's a culinary spectacle, making it a must-visit for anyone seeking the pinnacle of buffet dining in Seoul.

#### Cuisine and Highlights

The Parkview boasts an extensive selection that spans global culinary traditions. Guests can savor expertly prepared Western dishes, including prime cuts of steak and pasta, alongside a rich array of Japanese sushi and sashimi, freshly prepared at live stations. The Korean section is particularly noteworthy, featuring traditional favorites like flavorful marinated grilled beef (Bulgogi), various stews, and a diverse range of fermented cabbage dishes (Kimchi). The seafood selection is incredibly fresh, often including seasonal delights. Don't forget the dessert bar, which is a feast for the eyes and the palate, featuring patisserie-quality cakes, tarts, and fresh fruits.

#### Ambiance and Experience

The restaurant's design is sleek and modern, featuring floor-to-ceiling windows that offer picturesque views of Namsan Mountain and the hotel's lush sculpture garden. The atmosphere is elegant yet comfortable, making it ideal for special occasions, business dinners, or a luxurious treat. Service is impeccable, with attentive staff ensuring a seamless dining experience.

*   **Weekday Dinner:** Approximately KRW 180,000 - 200,000 per person
*   **Weekend Lunch/Dinner:** Approximately KRW 200,000 - 220,000 per person
(Prices are estimates and subject to change; excluding beverages)

#### Location and Contact

*   **Location:** The Shilla Seoul, 249 Dongho-ro, Jung-gu, Seoul
*   **Best For:** Luxury dining, business meals, special occasions
*   **Insider Tip:** Reservations are highly recommended, especially for weekend dinner and during peak seasons. Consider visiting for lunch for a slightly less crowded experience and a marginally lower price point.

The buffet features distinct sections: a vibrant salad bar, an impressive array of cold cuts and cheeses, a dedicated Asian corner with Chinese and Southeast Asian specialties, and a robust Western section with carving stations. The seafood spread is always fresh and abundant, and their dessert station is known for its exquisite handmade chocolates and pastries. The presentation of each dish is as impressive as its taste, reflecting the hotel's commitment to culinary excellence.

### 3. Grand Hyatt Seoul The Terrace: Scenic Views with Global Flavors

Perched on Namsan Mountain, Grand Hyatt Seoul's The Terrace offers more than just an exceptional buffet; it provides a dining experience with breathtaking panoramic views of the Han River and the Seoul skyline. This restaurant is celebrated for its commitment to freshness, showcasing a diverse international menu in an elegant, sun-drenched setting.

#### Cuisine and Highlights

The Terrace specializes in a broad range of international cuisines, prepared in open kitchens, allowing guests to interact with chefs. Expect a comprehensive selection of Korean dishes, from savory pancakes (Jeon) to various rice and noodle options, alongside expertly crafted Japanese sushi and tempura. The Western section includes a popular grill station offering various meats and seafood cooked to perfection, while the European deli section provides an assortment of cheeses and charcuterie. Their fresh fruit and juice bar is a refreshing addition, complementing a dessert spread that includes both classic and contemporary choices.

#### Ambiance and Experience

The restaurant's design maximizes its stunning location, with large windows overlooking the city. The spacious, airy interior creates a relaxed yet sophisticated atmosphere. It’s particularly beautiful during sunset, offering a memorable backdrop to your meal. The Terrace is suitable for both casual family gatherings and more formal occasions, known for its warm service and inviting environment.

*   **Weekend Lunch/Dinner:** Approximately KRW 170,000 - 190,000 per person

### 4. Viking's Wharf (COEX or Jamsil): The Ultimate Seafood Extravaganza

For seafood aficionados, Viking's Wharf stands out as Seoul's premier all-you-can-eat seafood buffet. With multiple locations, including popular spots in COEX Mall and Lotte World Mall in Jamsil, it offers a truly magnificent spread of ocean's bounty, ensuring a fresh and diverse seafood experience that is hard to match. This establishment redefines the seafood buffet with its focus on premium quality and an interactive dining format.

#### Cuisine and Highlights

Viking's Wharf operates on a unique "market price" concept, aiming to deliver the freshest seafood available. The highlight is undoubtedly their live lobster station, where you can order a whole grilled lobster. Beyond lobster, the buffet features an extensive array of fresh sashimi and sushi, snow crab legs, king crab (seasonal), various types of grilled fish, and a selection of shellfish cooked to order. In addition to seafood, they offer a few non-seafood options, a generous salad bar, and a popular dessert section featuring artisanal ice cream from a famous Korean brand. The sheer volume and quality of seafood are what truly set Viking's Wharf apart.

#### Ambiance and Experience

The ambiance is bustling and lively, reminiscent of a vibrant seafood market, but with a refined touch. It's a family-friendly environment that can get quite energetic, especially during peak hours. The focus is clearly on the food, with efficient service ensuring tables are cleared and orders are taken promptly.

#### Estimated Pricing (2026)

*   **Fixed Price (Lunch & Dinner, Weekday & Weekend):** Approximately KRW 130,000 - 150,000 per person
(Prices are estimates and subject to change; excluding beverages; often includes some basic drinks)

#### Location and Contact

*   **Location Examples:** COEX Mall (513 Yeongdong-daero, Gangnam-gu, Seoul) or Lotte World Mall (300 Olympic-ro, Songpa-gu, Seoul)
*   **Best For:** Seafood lovers, groups, families
*   **Insider Tip:** Reservations are absolutely essential, often needing to be made weeks in advance for weekend slots. Go with an empty stomach and prioritize your must-try items, especially the lobster, as portions can be generous.

### Frequently Asked Questions about Seoul's All-You-Can-Eat Buffets

**Q1: Is it necessary to make reservations for these buffets?**
A1: For the premium hotel buffets (Shilla, JW Marriott, Grand Hyatt) and especially for Viking's Wharf, reservations are highly recommended, often essential, particularly for dinner and weekend slots. For Ashley Queens, while not always strictly necessary, reservations can help avoid waiting, especially during peak meal times.

**Q2: What is the typical dress code for these restaurants?**
A2: For the luxury hotel buffets (Shilla, JW Marriott, Grand Hyatt), a smart casual dress code is generally appropriate. This means no shorts, flip-flops, or overly casual sportswear. For Viking's Wharf and Ashley Queens, the dress code is more relaxed, but smart casual is still recommended for a comfortable dining experience.

**Q3: Are there vegetarian or vegan options available at these buffets?**
A3: Most international buffets, especially the hotel ones, offer a wide variety of dishes that include significant salad bars, fresh fruits, some vegetable-based Korean sides, and sometimes specific vegetarian hot dishes. However, dedicated vegan options might be limited, and cross-contamination is possible. It's always best to call ahead and inquire about specific dietary needs if you have strict requirements.

**Q4: Is tipping customary in Seoul, particularly at buffets?**
A4: Tipping is generally not customary or expected in South Korea. The service charge is usually included in the price, especially at high-end establishments. You do not need to leave an additional tip.

**Q5: How can I reach these buffet restaurants using public transportation?**
A5: Seoul boasts an excellent public transportation system (subway and bus). Most of these hotels and malls are conveniently located near subway stations. For instance, JW Marriott Seoul is directly connected to Express Bus Terminal Station. The Shilla Seoul and Grand Hyatt Seoul are typically a short taxi ride from nearby subway stations, or the hotels may offer shuttle services. Utilizing navigation apps like KakaoMap or Naver Map (which are more accurate in Korea than Google Maps for public transport) is highly recommended for real-time directions.
