## Financials: Budgeting & Tax Refunds

**Garosu Gil boutique shopping in Sinsa Dong** is not a budget destination, but that doesn't mean you can't save money.

### Tax Refunds
Most boutiques with the "Global Tax Free" or "Global Blue" logo in the window offer immediate tax refunds.
*   **Threshold:** You must spend a minimum of **â‚©30,000** in a single transaction.
*   **Process:** Present your physical passport at the counter. The tax amount (approx. 6-7%) is deducted instantly from your total bill.
*   **Note:** If the store only gives you a receipt for a refund, you must claim it at the airport kiosks or major city refund points.

## The Vintage Scene in Sinsa

While known for "new," Sinsa has a growing luxury vintage market. Stores like **9 Owl** and various pop-ups sell vintage Chanel, Dior, and YSL.
*   **Authenticity:** Japan and Korea have very strict laws regarding counterfeits, making vintage shopping relatively safe here.
*   **Condition:** Korean vintage is notoriously well-kept. "Mint condition" usually means it looks brand new.
*   **Price:** Do not expect thrift store prices. A vintage Chanel jacket might still cost â‚©2,000,000, but it is a curated piece.

## Practical Tips for the Best Experience

1.  **Time it Right:** Avoid Mondays. Many smaller boutiques and galleries close on Mondays. The best time to visit is Wednesday or Thursday afternoon to beat the weekend crowds but ensure everything is open.
2.  **Wear Comfortable Shoes:** You will do a lot of walking. The "Sinsa Strut" is famous, but the pavement can be uneven in the alleys.
3.  **Restrooms:** Finding public restrooms is tough. Use the restrooms in larger buildings like the Apple Store, Garosu-gil Sinsa Square, or cafes where you purchase a drink.
4.  **Wi-Fi:** Most cafes and large stores offer free, high-speed Wi-Fi.
