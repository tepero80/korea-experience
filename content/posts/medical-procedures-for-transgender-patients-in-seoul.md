## Facial Feminization Surgery (FFS) in Korea

Facial Feminization Surgery is not a single procedure but a collection of surgeries designed to soften masculine facial features and create a more feminine appearance. Seoul is arguably the best place in the world for the "bone work" aspect of FFS.

### The "V-Line" and contouring mastery
The "V-Line" surgery, which combines mandible (jaw) reduction with genioplasty (chin surgery), is the most requested procedure. Korean surgeons specialize in creating a smooth, tapered jawline without the "stepped" look that can occur with less experienced techniques.

*   **Duration:** 2 to 3 hours
*   **Anesthesia:** General Anesthesia
*   **Hospitalization:** 1 night required
*   **Stitch Removal:** 7 to 14 days

The cost for V-Line surgery in Seoul typically ranges from **$6,000 to $10,000 USD** (approx. 8-â‚©13 million). This is significantly lower than the **$15,000 to $20,000 USD** often charged in the United States or Europe.

### Forehead reduction and brow bone shaving
The forehead is a major gender marker. Males typically have a protruding brow ridge and a higher hairline. Korean techniques often involve an endoscopic brow lift combined with brow bone shaving (or type 3 reconstruction for thicker bones) and hairline lowering.

Because hair transplantation is also a major industry in Korea, many clinics offer simultaneous hairline lowering via skin excision or hair grafts to round out the hairline, creating a softer frame for the face.

## Voice Feminization Surgery (VFS)

While Thailand is famous for SRS, South Korea holds the crown for Voice Feminization Surgery, largely due to the pioneering work of clinics like the Yeson Voice Center.

The "Yeson technique" (Vocal Fold Shortening and Retrodisplacement of the Anterior Commissure) is a non-incisional surgery. This means there is no cut on the neck, leaving no scarâ€”a crucial factor for many trans women who wish to avoid the visible "tracheal shave" scar line or simply want a stealth recovery.

### The procedure details
*   **Technique:** Endoscopic (through the mouth)
*   **Time:** Approximately 60 to 90 minutes
*   **Hospital Stay:** Not usually required (outpatient), or 1 night for observation
*   **Cost:** **$7,500 to $9,500 USD** (approx. 10-â‚©12.5 million)

## Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT) for travelers

International patients often worry about accessing hormones while in Korea for surgery.

**The short answer:** You cannot simply buy testosterone or estrogen over the counter. You must see a doctor.

### How to get a prescription
1.  **Bring your home prescription:** Bring a notarized English letter from your doctor at home stating your diagnosis (Gender Dysphoria) and current regimen.
2.  **Visit a global clinic:** Visit the International Health Care Center at major hospitals (like Severance or Asan) or an expat-friendly clinic in Itaewon.
3.  **Cost:** A consultation is roughly **30,000 to â‚©50,000** ($25-$40 USD).
4.  **Medication Cost:** Hormones are relatively affordable. A month's supply of oral estrogen or blockers might cost **20,000 to â‚©40,000** ($15-$30 USD). Injectable testosterone is available but requires a doctor's visit for administration in some cases.

## Costs and financial planning

Seoul is not a "budget" destination like some developing nations, but it offers "value luxury." You get top-tier safety and results for 30-50% less than the US.

### The Tax Refund
A critical financial benefit in Korea is the **Tax Refund for Cosmetic Surgery**. International patients staying less than 90 days are eligible for a VAT refund (approx. 7-8% of the surgery cost).
*   **Eligibility:** Must be done at a registered medical institution.
*   **Process:** The clinic gives you a "Certificate of Selling Medical Service." You scan this at the airport kiosk or tax refund counter before departing.
*   **Example:** On a $20,000 FFS package, you could get back roughly **$1,400 to $1,600 USD**.

## Safety, Legalities, and Emergency Contacts

Korea takes patient safety seriously. Operating rooms must have CCTV (by law as of recently) to prevent "ghost surgery" (where a different doctor operates than the one hired).

*   **Ghost Surgery Protections:** Explicitly ask if the clinic has CCTV in the OR and if you can verify it if necessary.
*   **Emergency Numbers:**
    *   **119:** Ambulance/Fire (Interpretation available)
    *   **1339:** Korea Disease Control (Medical advice/Hospital finder)
