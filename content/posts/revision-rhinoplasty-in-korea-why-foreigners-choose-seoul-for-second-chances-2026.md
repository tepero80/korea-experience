## Surgical Techniques: The Move Toward "Autologous" Materials

A major shift in Korean rhinoplasty trends for 2026 is the decline of artificial foreign bodies in revision cases and the rise of autologous (your own body's) tissues.

In the past, L-shaped silicone implants were common, but they carried a higher risk of extrusion and capsular contracture (where the nose tightens and turns upward like a pigâ€™s snout). Today, nearly all top-tier revision specialists in Seoul prefer **Autologous Rib Cartilage (Costal Cartilage)**.

### Why Rib Cartilage?
If you have already used your septal (nose) and auricular (ear) cartilage in a previous surgery, the rib is the last abundant source of strong cartilage.
1.  **Volume:** It provides enough material to completely rebuild the nasal bridge and tip.
2.  **Strength:** It is strong enough to push down a contracted, short nose.
3.  **Safety:** Since it is your own tissue, the rejection rate is effectively zero.

The harvesting procedure involves a small 2-3cm incision just below the breast line. While this sounds daunting, recovery is manageable, usually involving soreness similar to a heavy gym workout for 3-4 days.

## Recovery in Seoul: What to Expect

Recovery from revision surgery is often physically easier than the primary surgery regarding pain, but mentally tougher due to anxiety about the result. Swelling is the biggest adversary.

### Managing Swelling (Deswelling)
Korean post-op care is world-class. Most surgery packages include "Deswelling Treatments" such as:
*   **Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy:** Increases oxygen to tissues to speed up healing.
*   **Omnilux/LED Light Therapy:** Reduces inflammation.
*   **Pumpkin Juice:** The holy grail of Korean post-op recovery. You will find pouches of *hobak-jeup* (pumpkin juice) in every pharmacy.

## Legal and Visa Requirements 2026

Entering Korea for medical purposes has been streamlined. While many tourists enter on a standard tourist visa (B-2 or K-ETA), applying for a **C-3-3 Medical Tourism Visa** offers benefits, especially if your recovery takes longer than expected.

*   **Eligibility:** Must be invited by a registered medical facilitator or hospital.
*   **Duration:** 90 days (extendable in medical emergencies).
*   **Processing:** Faster than standard visas, usually 5-7 working days.

## Maximizing Your Recovery Diet

Your body needs high-quality fuel to repair tissue, especially after harvesting rib cartilage. Korean cuisine is generally healthy, but you should avoid spicy (capsaicin-heavy) foods for the first two weeks as they can induce runny noses and sneezing, which is dangerous for internal sutures.

Focus on soft, high-protein options. Seollangtang (Ox bone soup) is excellent for collagen, and Juk (rice porridge) is perfect for the first few days when chewing might be uncomfortable.
