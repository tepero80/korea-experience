## Etiquette and Tips for Working with Korean Shoppers

Korean business culture is polite and punctual. Here is how to be a great client.

1.  **Be Punctual:** Time is money. If you are 30 minutes late, that is 30 minutes deducted from your shopping time. Traffic in Seoul (especially Gangnam) is horrendous; take the subway.
2.  **Be Honest About Budget:** Do not be embarrassed if your budget is low. A good stylist can do wonders at Goto Mall (Express Bus Terminal underground shopping) with $100. Being vague about money wastes time.
3.  **Tipping:** Tipping is not mandatory in Korea. However, for personalized services like this, if the shopper went above and beyond (e.g., carrying heavy bags, fighting for a discount), buying them a coffee or a small gift (like a bakery item) is a culturally appropriate "thank you."
4.  **Comfortable Shoes:** You will walk 10,000+ steps. Do not wear heels.
5.  **Wear Easy Clothes:** You will be changing constantly. Wear separates that are easy to slip on and off. Avoid button-heavy shirts or lace-up boots.
