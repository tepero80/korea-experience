## Understanding the Korean Housing Market

Before downloading apps, it is critical to understand the unique rental systems in Korea, as every app categorizes listings based on these financial models. The market is not merely about "renting"; it is a choice between *Wolse* (monthly rent) and *Jeonse* (lump-sum deposit).

<InfoBox type="info" title="The Concept of Pyeong">

In 2026, the *Wolse* system remains the most accessible for foreigners. This typically involves a deposit (Key Money) ranging from 5 million to ‚Ç©20 million ($3,750 to $15,000 USD) and a monthly rent. Conversely, *Jeonse* requires a massive deposit‚Äîoften 50% to 70% of the property's market value‚Äîbut zero monthly rent. With current interest rates stabilizing, *Jeonse* deposits for a standard two-bedroom apartment in Seoul average around ‚Ç©300 million ($225,000 USD).

## Safety Checks and Red Flags

The Korean real estate market is generally safe, but rental scams involving deposit theft do happen. In 2025, the government cracked down heavily on "Jeonse Fraud," but caution is still required.

1.  **Certified Copy of Register (Deung-gi-bu-deung-bon):** Before signing anything, the agent must show you this document. It proves who the real owner is and if there are any heavy mortgages on the property. If the debt on the building is more than 70% of its value, walk away.
2.  **Identity Verification:** Ensure the name on the bank account where you send the deposit matches the name on the Title Deed exactly. Never send money to an agent's personal account.
3.  **Move-in Report (Jeon-ip-sin-go):** As soon as you move in, you must register your new address at the local community center (Dong-sami-so) or Immigration office. This gives you legal protection over your deposit.

<InfoBox type="danger" title="Critical Warning">
  Never sign a contract where the landlord refuses to let you do the 'Move-in Report' (Jeon-ip-sin-go). They may offer a discount on rent for this, but it means you have ZERO legal claim to your deposit if the building goes bankrupt.
</InfoBox>
