## The Titans of Hiking: Korea's Best Mountain Trails

For those willing to sweat for their views, Korea's mountainous terrain offers world-class hiking infrastructure. The contrast of grey granite peaks against fiery red maples and golden oaks is the quintessential image of Korean autumn.

### Seoraksan National Park (Gangwon-do)

Seoraksan is arguably the most famous mountain in Korea for autumn colors. Located near Sokcho on the east coast, it offers trails ranging from easy walks to grueling climbs. The "Dinosaur Ridge" (Gongnyong Ridge) is famous among hikers, but casual visitors should stick to the Osaek Mineral Spring course or the cable car route.

**The Ulsanbawi Rock Course:**
This is a challenging but rewarding hike. It involves a steep staircase climb (over 800 steps) towards the end.
*   **Distance:** 3.8km (one way)
*   **Duration:** 2 hours up, 1.5 hours down
*   **Elevation Gain:** ~873m
*   **Calorie Burn:** Approx. 850-1000 kcal

The view from the top offers a panoramic sweep of the East Sea and the autumn-covered valleys. It is visually stunning but physically demanding.

### Naejangsan National Park (Jeolla-do)

While Seoraksan is about grand peaks, Naejangsan is about the density of color. It is famous for its "baby maples," which have smaller leaves that turn a more intense crimson than standard maples. The park is home to the famous "Uhwa Pavilion," a small blue-tiled pavilion in the middle of a pond, surrounded by fiery red treesâ€”a photographer's dream.

<InfoBox type="warning" title="Crowd Control">
Naejangsan is incredibly popular domestically. During the peak weekend (usually the first weekend of November), over 100,000 visitors may enter the park. If possible, visit on a Tuesday or Wednesday to avoid "human traffic jams" on the trails.
</InfoBox>

**The Nature Trail:**
Unlike Seoraksan, the main draw here is the flat walking path from the ticket office to Naejangsa Temple.
*   **Distance:** 2.5km (one way)
*   **Duration:** 1 hour (walking slowly for photos)
*   **Difficulty:** Very Easy

### Bukhansan National Park (Seoul)

For those who don't want to leave the capital, Bukhansan offers legitimate mountain hiking accessible by subway. The Baegundae Peak offers a view of the entire city of Seoul framed by autumn colors.

*   **Access:** Subway Line 3 (Gupabal Station) or Line 4 (Suyu Station).
*   **Height:** 836m.
*   **Gear:** Hiking boots are highly recommended as the granite can be slippery.

## Photography Guide: Capturing the Colors

Shooting autumn colors in Korea presents unique challenges, primarily managing dynamic range (bright sky vs. dark shadows under trees) and crowds.

### Best Camera Settings for Foliage
*   **Polarizing Filter (CPL):** Essential. It cuts the glare off the waxy leaves, making the reds and yellows pop, and deepens the blue sky.
*   **White Balance:** Set to "Cloudy" or "Shade" (approx 6000K-6500K) to warm up the image and enhance the golden tones.
*   **Aperture:** Use f/8 to f/11 for landscapes to keep everything sharp. Use f/1.8 to f/2.8 for detailed shots of single leaves with bokeh.

### Composition Tips
1.  **Frame with Architecture:** Use the curved eaves of a Hanok (traditional house) to frame the colorful branches. This provides context that you are in Korea, not just "a forest."
2.  **Look Down:** Sometimes the carpet of fallen leaves on a stone temple floor is more evocative than the trees themselves.
3.  **Contrast:** Look for red maples against green pine trees or grey granite rocks.
