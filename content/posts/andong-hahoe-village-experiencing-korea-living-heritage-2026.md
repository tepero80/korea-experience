Andong Hahoe Village is often mistaken for a museum or a film set, but it is something far more rare: a living, breathing community that has maintained its architectural and cultural integrity for over 600 years. Located approximately 180 kilometers southeast of Seoul, this UNESCO World Heritage site is home to the Ryu clan of Pungsan, who still reside in the tile-roofed nobility houses and thatched-roof commoner dwellings that cluster within the S-shaped bend of the Nakdong River. ## Why Hahoe Village Matters in 2026

While South Korea rushes toward the future, Hahoe Village holds the line for the past. Unlike the Korean Folk Village in Yongin, which is a reconstructed open-air museum populated by actors, Hahoe is a residential space. As of January 2026, approximately 230 residents across 127 households call this village home. This authenticity was the primary reason Queen Elizabeth II visited in 1999 to celebrate her 73rd birthday, and why her son, Prince Andrew, returned in 2019.

The village's layout is dictated by *Pungsu-jiri* (Korean Feng Shui). It is shaped like a lotus flower floating on water, surrounded by the Nakdong River on three sides. This unique geography has protected it from invasions and modernization alike. The village is also the birthplace of renowned scholars of the Joseon period, including Ryu Un-ryong and Ryu Seong-ryong, the latter being a Prime Minister who served during the Imjin War (1592-1598).

When you walk the dirt paths of Hahoe, you are walking through a genealogical chart made of wood and stone. The tile-roofed houses (*waga*) at the center were for the aristocratic *yangban* class, while the thatched-roof houses (*choga*) radiating outward housed the servants and commoners. This visible class structure, preserved intact, is unique even within Korea.

## Cultural Immersion: Things to Do

Exploring Hahoe is less about checking off sights and more about soaking in the atmosphere. However, several key spots provide the framework for your visit.

### 1. The Hahoe Mask Dance (Hahoe Byeolsingut Talnori)
This is the absolute highlight of any trip to Andong. Designated as an Intangible Cultural Heritage, this dance drama originated in the 12th century as a ritual to appease local spirits. Unlike court dances which were refined and restrained, this village dance is satirical, mocking the arrogance of the ruling class (aristocrats and scholars) and the corruption of monks.

*   **Venue:** Hahoe Mask Dance Performance Hall (located before the shuttle bus zone).
*   **Time:** Daily at 2:00 PM (check seasonal schedules; often closed Mondays in winter).
*   **Duration:** 60 minutes.
*   **Cost:** Free (included in village admission).
*   **Language:** Performed in Korean, but screens usually display English, Chinese, and Japanese subtitles. The physical comedy transcends language barriers.

### 2. Man-song-jeong Pine Forest
Planted along the riverbank by Ryu Un-ryong in the 16th century, this forest of Mansong (10,000 pines) serves a dual purpose: it acts as a windbreak against the harsh river winds and completes the feng shui of the village by screening the rugged cliff face opposite. It is a serene walking path that stretches for about 400 meters along the sandy beach.

### 3. Buyongdae Cliff
Across the river from the Pine Forest looms Buyongdae, a 64-meter high cliff. The name means "Lotus Cliff." While you can admire it from the village, the best experience is climbing it for a panoramic view of the S-shaped village layout.
*   **Ferry:** A small traditional boat ferries visitors across the river.
*   **Cost:** â‚©5,000 round trip (cash only usually).
*   **Operation:** Subject to water levels and weather.
*   **Hike:** Once across, it's a short but steep 10-minute hike to the top. The view of the tile roofs interlocking like puzzle pieces is the definitive photo of Andong.

### 4. Historic Houses
Several major houses are open to the public, though many remain private residences.
*   **Chunghyodang:** The head house of the Ryu clan, built in memory of Ryu Seong-ryong. It displays his writings and relics.
*   **Yangjindang:** The oldest house in the village, representing unique architectural styles from the early Joseon period.

For those interested in how these locations relate to popular media, many visitors recognize the architecture from historical dramas. You can read more about [historic K-Drama sets](/blog/historic-kdrama-sets-folk-villages-and-palaces) to see how Hahoe compares to filming locations.

## The Culinary Landscape: What to Eat

Andong is a heavyweight in the Korean culinary scene. The food here is salty, spicy, and deeply traditional.

### 1. Andong Jjimdak (Braised Chicken)
You cannot visit Andong without eating Jjimdak. This dish consists of chicken braised in a soy-based sauce with glass noodles, potatoes, carrots, spinach, and dried chili peppers. It is savory, slightly sweet, and packs a spicy kick.
*   **Where:** The "Marketplace" area before the ticket booth has over a dozen restaurants serving this.
*   **Cost:** Large plate (serves 3-4) is around â‚©35,000 - â‚©40,000.
*   **Recommendation:** If you are heading back to the city center, visit the "Andong Old Market Jjimdak Street" for the most authentic experience. For detailed recommendations, see our specific guide on [Andong Jjimdak best restaurants](/blog/andong-jjimdak-best-restaurants-in-the-hometown).

### 2. Gan-godeungeo (Salted Mackerel)
Before refrigeration, fish had to be heavily salted to survive the transport from the coast to inland Andong. This preservation method created a local delicacy. The mackerel is grilled to a crispy perfection.
*   **Cost:** Set meal ~â‚©15,000 per person.

### 3. Heotjesabap
This translates to "fake memorial ceremony rice." In Confucian tradition, specific foods are prepared for ancestor worship (*jesa*). The scholars of Andong enjoyed these dishes so much they began making them even when there was no ceremony. It is a type of Bibimbap served with soy sauce instead of gochujang, accompanied by jeon (pancakes) and soup.
*   **Flavor profile:** Mild, earthy, and refined.

*   **Admission:** â‚©1,500.

### Woryeonggyo Bridge (Moonlight Bridge)
