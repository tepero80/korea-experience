Opened in 2021, it remains Seoul's largest department store, spanning a staggering 89,100 square meters.

## Timeless Romance: The Classic Locations

Some locations transcend specific shows. They are the go-to spots for directors looking to convey romance, heartbreak, or historical gravity.

### Namsan Seoul Tower (N Seoul Tower)
No K-Drama list is complete without Namsan Tower. From *Boys Over Flowers* to *True Beauty* and *My Love from the Star*, this is where couples lock their love (literally, with padlocks) or miss each other by mere seconds. The tower sits atop Namsan Mountain, rising 236 meters above the peak, providing a total elevation of 480 meters above sea level.

Reaching the tower is an adventure in itself. You cannot take a taxi or private car to the summit due to environmental regulations established in 2005. You must take the Namsan Cable Car or a dedicated circulation bus (01A or 01B).

### Deoksugung Stonewall Walkway
Known as the breakup spot due to an urban legend, this 900-meter scenic road became internationally famous thanks to *Goblin: The Lonely and Great God*. This is where the Goblin (Gong Yoo) meets the Grim Reaper (Lee Dong-wook) for the very first time.

The walkway follows the perimeter of Deoksugung Palace. Unlike other palaces, the stone wall here features a unique mix of traditional Korean architecture and early 20th-century western influences. The walkway is open 24 hours, but the best lighting for photography is during the "golden hour" (approx. 17:30 in spring/autumn).

## Historical Drama (Sageuk) Immersion

Seoul's palaces are the real-world sets for *The Red Sleeve*, *Kingdom*, and *Under the Queen's Umbrella*.

### Gyeongbokgung Palace
Built in 1395, this is the main royal palace of the Joseon dynasty. The Geunjeongjeon Hall is the massive structure seen in almost every royal court assembly scene. The Hyangwonjeong Pavilion, a two-story hexagonal pavilion built on a small island in a lake, is often the setting for secret royal meetings or romantic strolls.

The palace is massive, covering 410,000 square meters. Walking from the main gate (Gwanghwamun) to the rear garden takes about 20 minutes without stopping.

### Bukchon Hanok Village
Located between Gyeongbokgung and Changdeokgung palaces, this residential area features hundreds of traditional houses called *hanok*. It was the primary filming location for *Personal Taste* and serves as the backdrop for historical flashbacks in modern dramas like *Goblin*.

It is crucial to remember that this is a real residential neighborhood. The "quiet hours" policy is strictly enforced, and tourists are asked to keep noise levels down, particularly before 10:00 AM and after 5:00 PM.

## University Campuses: Youth and Nostalgia

University campuses in Seoul are architecturally stunning and serve as the setting for youth dramas like *Cheer Up*, *Reply 1994*, and *True Beauty*.

### Yonsei University (Sinchon Campus)
The Underwood Hall at Yonsei University is covered in ivy that changes color with the seasonsâ€”lush green in summer and fiery red in autumn. This Victorian-style building looks like it belongs in a Harry Potter movie but is a staple in K-Dramas representing elite education or historical settings.

It is located about 1.5 kilometers from Sinchon Station (Line 2). The walk from the station through the "Sinchon rotary" is vibrant, filled with busking performances and student cafes where an Americano averages â‚©4,500.

<InfoBox type="warning" title="Visitor Etiquette">

### Ewha Womans University
Famous for its "ECC" (Ewha Campus Complex), a massive glass and steel valley carved into the hillside. This futuristic location has appeared in dramas emphasizing modern architecture and high fashion. It is located just two subway stops away from Yonsei, making it easy to visit both in one afternoon (approx. 20 minutes travel time).

## Strategic Planning: The 2026 Drama Itinerary

To maximize your time, you should group locations by district. Seoul traffic can be unforgiving, with cross-town taxi rides taking over an hour during rush hour (08:00-09:30 and 18:00-19:30).

### North of the River (Gangbuk)
This is where history lives. Focus on Jongno-gu and Mapo-gu.
*   **Morning:** Gyeongbokgung Palace (wearing Hanbok).
*   **Lunch:** Samgyetang (Ginseng Chicken Soup) near Tosokchon, a legendary spot frequented by politicians and filmed in documentaries (approx. â‚©20,000 per bowl).
*   **Afternoon:** Bukchon Hanok Village and calm walk at Deoksugung Stone Wall.
*   **Evening:** Namsan Tower for sunset.

### South of the River (Gangnam)
This is for modern luxury and K-Pop vibes.
*   **Morning:** Starfield COEX Mall (Starfield Library). While busy, the towering bookshelves are iconic (seen in *Record of Youth*).
*   **Lunch:** Garosu-gil in Sinsa-dong for trendy pasta or fusion food.
*   **Afternoon:** Lotte World Tower and Seokchon Lake.
*   **Evening:** Banpo Bridge Rainbow Fountain (April-October). This is the setting for countless romantic confessions. The show runs for 20 minutes every 30 minutes starting at 19:30.
