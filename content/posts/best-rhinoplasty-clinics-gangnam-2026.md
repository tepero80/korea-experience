## Common Rhinoplasty Techniques in Korea

### **1. Autologous Cartilage Rhinoplasty**

**What It Is:** Uses your own cartilage (from ear or rib) instead of silicone implants.

**Pros:**
- Zero risk of implant rejection
- Most natural feel
- Lasts forever (no need for replacement)

**Cons:**
- Longer surgery time
- Additional incision scar
- Higher cost

**Best For:** Patients with very thin skin or previous implant complications

---

### **2. Silicone Implant Rhinoplasty**

**What It Is:** FDA-approved solid silicone implant inserted to augment bridge.

**Pros:**
- Shorter surgery time
- Lower cost
- Predictable results

**Cons:**
- 5-10% risk of implant shift/infection
- May need replacement after 10-15 years

**Best For:** First-time rhinoplasty patients seeking modest enhancement

---

### **3. Gore-Tex Rhinoplasty**

**What It Is:** Expanded polytetrafluoroethylene (ePTFE) implant - softer than silicone.

**Pros:**
- Tissue integration (body grows into material)
- More natural feel than silicone
- Lower infection rate

**Cons:**
- More expensive than silicone
- Harder to remove if revision needed

**Best For:** Patients wanting implant durability with natural feel

---

### **4. Preservation Rhinoplasty**

**What It Is:** NEW technique (popular since 2023) that preserves nasal structure instead of breaking bones.

**Pros:**
- Minimal swelling and bruising
- Faster recovery (5-7 days)
- More natural breathing function retained

**Cons:**
- Limited availability (only advanced surgeons)
- Can't correct severe deformities

**Best For:** Subtle refinements, younger patients

## FAQs: Rhinoplasty in Gangnam

### **Q: Is it safe to travel internationally so soon after surgery?**

**A:** Most clinics clear patients for air travel **10-14 days** post-op. Flying too early can increase swelling. Always:
- Book aisle seats for easy movement
- Use saline spray during flight
- Avoid heavy lifting of luggage
- Wear compression garment if recommended
