## STEP-BY-STEP GUIDE

**Guide Title:** How to Master Remote Queuing in Seoul (Without a Korean ID)
**Total Time:** 15 Minutes (Setup)
**Difficulty:** Medium

### Step 1: Pre-Trip Setup (Home Country)
Download **Catch Table Global** (ensure it's the "Global" version with the English logo). Create an account using your Apple ID or Google Account. **Do not** try to download the Korean version of Tabling yet; it will just frustrate you. Link your credit card immediately so you don't fumble with it when reservations open at midnight.
> **Pro Tip:** Turn on *all* notifications for this app. If you miss the "Come in now" alert, they will skip you in 3 minutes flat.

### Step 2: The "010" SIM Card Strategy
When you land at Incheon, buy a SIM card that includes a **010 phone number** (Voice + Data), not just Data-only. You cannot verify your identity with this prepaid number for the *Tabling* app, BUT you can input this number at physical kiosks to get SMS alerts.
> **Pro Tip:** Take a photo of your new Korean phone number and save it as your lock screen so you can type it into kiosks quickly.

### Step 3: Remote Queuing (The "Open Run")
For super-hot spots like *London Bagel Museum*, open Catch Table Global at 8:59 AM. Refresh the page exactly at 9:00 AM. Click "Join Waitlist." You will be assigned a number (e.g., #45). You can now chill at your hotel until the number drops to #5.
> **Pro Tip:** If the app says "Remote Waiting Not Available," it means the queue is already full or they only accept walk-ins.

### Step 4: The Physical Tabling Kiosk (The Backup)
If a restaurant isn't on Catch Table, go to the front door. Look for a tablet (usually iPad-sized) with the "Tabling" logo. Switch the language to English (if available) or look for the button saying "Waitlist Registration." Enter your **010** prepaid number.
> **Pro Tip:** If you bought a data-only SIM and have no number, you are stuck. You must ask the staff to manually write your name down, but many busy places will refuse this in 2026.

## FAQ

### Q1: Can I use the Tabling app without a Korean Alien Registration Card (ARC)?
**No, not for the full features.** You can download it and browse, but you cannot verify your identity to join remote queues. You are essentially locked out of the "remote" features and must use the physical kiosks.

### Q2: Why does my prepaid SIM card not work for identity verification?
**Because it's not a "Real Name" verification.** In Korea, identity verification links your phone number to your government ID (ARC) and bank account. A prepaid tourist SIM is just a temporary number; it doesn't have the legal "digital footprint" required to verify your identity on apps like Naver or Tabling.

### Q3: What is "Catch Table Global"?
It is the international version of Korea's popular reservation app. It was built specifically for tourists, allowing sign-ups via email and payments via international credit cards. It is your lifeline.

### Q4: Is "London Bagel Museum" really worth the 3-hour wait?
**Honestly? Yes and No.** The bagels are great, but no bread is worth standing on a sidewalk for 3 hours. However, if you use **remote queuing** on Catch Table to wait from your hotel bed, then yes, it is absolutely worth it.

### Q5: Do I get my deposit back?
**Yes.** The deposit is usually refunded to your card *after* you show up and pay for your meal. Sometimes they deduct the meal cost from the deposit, but usually, it's a full refund + new charge.

The local app requires identity verification that is tied to a Korean telecom contract, which is a firewall you can't breach with a tourist SIM [cite: 6, 7].

Here is the deal: Download **Catch Table Global** immediately. It is the designated lifeline for travelers in 2026. ### Section 2: The Background Story

You might be wondering, "Why is eating dinner so complicated?" It wasn't always this way. Back in the day, you just showed up. But post-pandemic, Seoul developed an intense "Waiting Culture." It became a status symbol to wait three hours for a bagel. Seriously, people wear it like a badge of honor [cite: 3].

By 2023, apps like **Tabling** and **Catch Table** had taken over. They replaced the clipboard with digital kiosks. But this created a new problem: Scalpers. People started booking popular slots and selling them online. To fight this, the Korean tech giants locked everything down. They introduced "Real Name Verification" (identity checks) to ensure that the person booking the table was a real human with a real Korean ID [cite: 9, 10].

This was great for locals but a disaster for tourists. You‚Äôd show up, hungry and excited, only to find you couldn't even join the line because you didn't have a Korean ID.

Then came 2025. The Fair Trade Commission (FTC) stepped in to fix another problem: No-shows. People were booking five restaurants and only showing up to one. The FTC updated the regulations, allowing restaurants (especially fine dining and Omakase) to charge a "No-Show Penalty" of up to **40% of the meal price** [cite: 1, 2]. This made the reservation systems even stricter about payments and deposits. So here we are in 2026‚Äîa high-tech, high-stakes dining landscape where you need the right app or you don't eat.

### Section 5: The Stuff Nobody Tells You

Here are the edge cases that will trip you up if you aren't prepared.

**The "010" SIM Card Nuance**
I cannot stress this enough: When you buy a SIM card at the airport (LG U+, KT, SKT), pay the extra $5 for the one with a **Phone Number (Voice/Text)**. Even though you can't use it for identity verification online, *physical kiosks* don't check your ID‚Äîthey just need a valid 10-digit number to send a text to. If you have a number, you can use 99% of kiosks. If you have data-only, you are playing on Hard Mode [cite: 12, 15].

**The "0-Cha" Culture**
You will hear Koreans talking about "1-cha" (first round/dinner) and "2-cha" (second round/drinks). But in 2026, we have "0-cha." This is the place you go *while* you wait. Do not stand outside the restaurant like a lost puppy. Koreans book the restaurant, then immediately find a trendy cafe next door (the 0-cha) to wait out the 90 minutes in comfort. It's part of the date. Embrace it [cite: 3].

**The "No-Show" Financial Trap**
This is serious. In late 2025, the government got tired of restaurant owners losing money. They authorized penalties up to **40%**. I had a friend who booked an Omakase for four people. Total bill was going to be $600. They forgot to cancel and just didn't go. They woke up to a **$240 charge** on their card. Catch Table is brutal about this because they have your credit card on file. If you aren't going, cancel at least 24 hours in advance [cite: 1, 2].

**The "Sold Out" Notification**
Sometimes, you will be waiting in the remote queue, you are #5 in line, and suddenly you get a notification: "Ingredient Sold Out." It happens. High-demand spots like *Mongtan* run out of ribs. The app will boot you from the queue. Have a backup plan (Plan B) nearby so you aren't left wandering hungry [cite: 16].
