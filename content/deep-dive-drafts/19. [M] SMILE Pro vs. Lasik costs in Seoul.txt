This volume allows for a unique economic model where patients can access the world's most advanced laser systems at prices comparable to those in developing markets like India or Thailand, despite the utilization of premium German and American hardware.Technical Paradigms: SMILE Pro vs. LASIK ArchitectureThe choice between SMILE Pro and traditional LASIK in 2026 is no longer a simple binary based on cost, but a complex clinical decision involving corneal topography, epithelial thickness mapping, and lifestyle requirements. LASIK remains the standard for those requiring topography-guided treatments, while SMILE Pro has emerged as the preferred choice for those prioritizing rapid recovery and minimal dry eye risk.The Mechanism of Action: Flap vs. LenticuleLASIK is a two-step process. The first step involves the creation of a 20 mm circular corneal flap, typically 100 to 120 microns thick, using a femtosecond laser. This flap is then reflected to allow an excimer laser to ablate the stromal tissue according to a pre-programmed wavefront or topography map. In contrast, SMILE Pro is a "keyhole" procedure. It utilizes a single femtosecond laser—the VisuMax 800—to create a microscopic lens-shaped piece of tissue (lenticule) within the intact cornea. This lenticule is subsequently removed through a tiny 2 mm to 3 mm incision.The clinical implications of these different mechanisms are profound. The 20 mm flap in LASIK requires the severing of thousands of corneal nerves, which are essential for the feedback loop that triggers tear production. SMILE Pro’s micro-incision preserves roughly 80% more of the corneal sub-basal nerve plexus, which correlates directly with a lower incidence of postoperative dry eye syndrome. Furthermore, the absence of a flap eliminates the risk of flap-related complications such as dislocation, folding, or epithelial ingrowth, making SMILE Pro the superior choice for patients involved in contact sports or professions with high risk of ocular trauma.VisuMax 800: The SMILE Pro EvolutionThe VisuMax 800 system introduced in the 2023-2024 cycle has become the industry standard for premium clinics in Seoul by 2026.
