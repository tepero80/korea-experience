## FAQ

### Q1: Is drinking alcohol at the Han River legal?
Yes! Despite some scary headlines in 2023 about banning alcohol, it is still legal to drink beer/soju at the river as of 2026. However, you cannot drink excessively or cause a scene, and there are specific "alcohol-free" designated zones near playgrounds‚Äîjust avoid those.

### Q2: What if I can't find the Delivery Zone?
Look for a cluster of motorcycles and a large crowd of people staring at their phones. In Yeouido, **Zone 2** is near the Mini Stop and the E-Land Cruise terminal. In Banpo, it's near the roundabout by the subway exit. If lost, ask a young local: "Baedal Zone uh-dee-eh-yo?" (Where is the delivery zone?).

### Q3: Can I order pizza instead of chicken?
Absolutely. Pizza, Jjajangmyeon (noodles), and even Tteokbokki are popular. However, "Chimaek" (Chicken + Beer) is the cultural standard. Just be aware that noodles get soggy fast, so pizza or chicken are the safest delivery foods.

### Q4: Do I need to tip the driver?
No. Tipping is not customary in Korea. The delivery fee (usually ‚Ç©3,000-‚Ç©5,000) covers it. If the weather is absolutely terrible (heavy rain), the app might charge a surcharge, but you don't hand cash to the driver unless you chose "Pay with Cash" (which you shouldn't do).

### Q5: What happens with the trash?
You must separate it. Major parks have huge waste stations. Liquids go in one bucket, food waste in another, cans/bottles in recycling, and general trash in the general bin. If you have the reusable containers (Zero Waste initiative), you must scan and return them to the specific return machines.

### Section 1: Answer the Main Question First!

Look, I know why you're here. You want to know if you can sit by the Han River, tap a few buttons on your phone, and have crispy Korean fried chicken magically appear in your hands. The answer is **YES**, but with a huge asterisk for 2026. You can no longer just sit anywhere and expect the driver to find you via GPS. Here is the deal: There are specific pickup points‚Äîliterally signs stuck in the ground‚Äîscattered around the major parks like Yeouido and Banpo. If you are in Yeouido, you are likely looking for **Delivery Zone 2**, which is right near the main subway exit. It costs about **‚Ç©25,000 ($19)** for a whole chicken, and yes, thanks to recent updates, you can finally do this without a Korean bank account!

### Section 2: The Background Story

So, how did we get here? A decade ago, the Han River parks were like the Wild West of food delivery. Motorcycles were zooming across the grass, dodging frisbees and terrified pedestrians, just to drop off a box of chicken directly to someone's tent. It was chaotic, it was dangerous, and honestly, it was kind of awesome‚Äîuntil the city realized it was a safety nightmare.

In 2016, the Seoul Metropolitan Government said "enough" and established the **Delivery Zone system**. They forced the bikes to stay on the paved roads and designated specific meeting points. It was annoying at first, but it made the parks much safer.

Fast forward to the post-pandemic era (2022-2024), and things got tricky for tourists. The apps required rigorous identity verification (the dreaded ARC) that locked out visitors. But here is the good news: recognizing the massive influx of tourists, major apps like **Baedal Minjok (Baemin)** and **Coupang Eats** launched massive updates between 2024 and 2026. They finally added English interfaces and foreign card support. Now, the barrier to entry is lower than ever, provided you know where to stand!

### Section 3: Breaking Down Your Options

Okay, so which app should you actually use? You have three main contenders in 2026, and the choice depends entirely on your comfort level with tech.

**Option A: Shuttle Delivery (The Tourist Safe Haven)**
If you are nervous about technology or just landed yesterday, download **Shuttle**. It was built for expats. The interface is perfect English, the customer service speaks English, and you can pay with PayPal or any foreign credit card without weird verification texts. The downside? It has fewer restaurants than the local giants, and the delivery fees can be slightly higher. But for peace of mind? It's unbeatable.

**Option B: Baedal Minjok / Baemin (The Local King)**
This is the app you see everyone in K-dramas using. As of 2025/2026, they rolled out a multilingual update (English, Chinese, Japanese) and started accepting foreign cards. This is huge because it opens up *every* restaurant in Seoul to you, not just the expat-friendly ones. The "Guest Mode" works, but sometimes the map pinning can be a bit finicky for non-Koreans. Use this if you want maximum variety (like that specific spicy Tteokbokki place).

**Option C: The "Human" Method (Concierge Services)**
If the apps are glitching (it happens), look up services like **Help Me Emo** or **Go Wonderfully** on KakaoTalk. These are real humans. You text them, "I'm at Yeouido Zone 2, I want BHC Chicken," and they place the order for you. You pay them via PayPal. It costs a small fee, but it's foolproof.

### Section 4: The Complete How-To

Let‚Äôs get practical. Here is exactly how to execute the perfect Chimaek picnic at **Yeouido Hangang Park** (the most popular spot).

**Step 1: The Setup**
Take the subway to **Yeouinaru Station (Line 5)** and go out **Exit 2**. As soon as you surface, you'll see people selling silver mats. Buy one (about ‚Ç©4,000) or rent one. Walk down the stairs toward the river. Do NOT go too far yet. Look for the sign that says **"Delivery Zone 2"**. It‚Äôs usually near the Mini Stop convenience store.

**Step 2: The Order**
Open your app. For the address, do not try to type "Han River." You need to enter the specific address for Zone 2: *330 Yeouidong-ro, Yeongdeungpo-gu*. If using Shuttle, it might just let you select "Yeouido Zone 2" from a list. Select your chicken (Boneless is key! Less trash!).

**Step 3: The Wait**
Go find a spot on the grass *near* the zone but not *in* it. Spread your mat. Go to the convenience store and buy your beer (or cola) and cups of ice while you wait. Keep your phone in your hand.

**Step 4: The Handoff**
When the app says "Arriving in 5 minutes," walk to the Zone. You will see a wall of motorcycle helmets. The drivers usually shout the last 4 digits of your phone number or the order number. If you don't speak Korean, just show them your phone screen. They are used to this. Grab the bag, check for the "service" (free soda or radish), and head back to your mat to feast.

### Section 5: The Stuff Nobody Tells You

Here are the insider tips that will save you from looking like a total rookie.

Seoul is serious about "Zero Waste." In 2026, many delivery orders at the Han River now come in stainless steel bowls instead of disposable plastic. **The Alcohol Rules**
You might have read articles from 2023 saying alcohol was going to be banned. As of right now, that was mostly put on hold due to public backlash. You *can* drink, but be smart.
