## FAQ

### Q1: Is it weird to eat alone at a fancy restaurant in Seoul?
No, not anymore. While it might have been unusual 10 years ago, the explosion of single-person households (Hon-bap culture) has normalized it. High-end restaurants usually have bar/counter seating specifically for this. You will likely see other solo diners next to you.

### Q2: Do I need to tip in fine dining restaurants in Korea?
Absolutely not. Tipping is not a thing in Korea, even at 3-star Michelin establishments. In fact, leaving money on the table can be seen as confusing or even rude (like you think they need charity). The service charge is already included in the bill.

### Q3: What if the menu says "2 servings minimum" (2in-bun)?
This is common in BBQ or Stew places. As a solo traveler, you have two choices: find a "Hon-bap" friendly spot (like Hongojib) or simply pay for two servings. If you order 2 servings of meat, they will happily seat you alone. You don't have to *eat* it all, but you have to *pay* for it.

### Q4: Can I accommodate dietary restrictions like vegetarian/vegan?
Yes, but you must give advance notice. Places like Mingles and Jungsik have specific vegetarian menus if booked in advance. However, at casual BBQ or traditional soup places, removing meat/fish broth is very difficult. Stick to Temple Cuisine (Sanchon, Balwoo Gongyang) for safe, high-end vegan food.

### Q5: How much should I budget for a fine dining trip?
For 2026, budget significantly more than old blog posts suggest. A dinner at a starred restaurant is now **â‚©300,000+ ($220 USD)**. A decent Hanwoo beef meal is **â‚©150,000+ ($110 USD)**. Even casual meals are hitting **â‚©15,000 ($11 USD)**. For a 3-day foodie trip hitting one fine dining spot and nice casual meals, budget around **$500-$600 USD** just for food.
