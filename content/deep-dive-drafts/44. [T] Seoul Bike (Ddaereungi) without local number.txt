## FAQ

### Q1: Can I use the Climate Card for bikes as a tourist?
The short answer is: It's complicated and usually **no**. While the 30-day Climate Card (~65,000 KRW) includes bikes, activating the bike benefit requires registering the card on the T-Money GO app, which requires a local phone number for identity verification. Stick to buying the 1,000 KRW vouchers.

### Q2: What if I crash the bike?
You are responsible. The Terms of Service state that users are liable for damages caused by negligence. However, for minor wear and tear, it's usually fine. If you are in a collision involving a person or car, do not leave the scene. Call 112 (Police) or 1330 (Tourist Helpline) immediately.

### Q3: Can I leave the bike anywhere when I'm done?
**NO.** This is not a dockless system like Lime or Bird. You **MUST** return it to an official Ddaereungi station. If you leave it on a random street corner, the timer keeps running, and you will be charged maximum fees and potentially banned/fined.

### Q4: My credit card isn't working on the app. What now?
This happens. Try using the website (bikeseoul.com) on your phone browser instead of the app for paymentâ€”sometimes the web gateway is more forgiving. Alternatively, the "Discover Seoul Pass" includes bike rentals, so if you have that pass, you can use it to redeem a voucher code.

### Q5: Are there helmets provided?
Generally, no. You are legally required to wear a helmet while cycling in Korea, but enforcement is very lax for Ddaereungi riders, and helmets are rarely provided at stations due to hygiene/theft issues. Ride carefully!
