## KEY TAKEAWAYS

**1. The "Queens Department Store" is actually The Hyundai Seoul.**
Here's the deal: The massive, futuristic mall where Hyun-woo works and Hae-in bosses everyone around is **The Hyundai Seoul** in Yeouido. **2. That wedding venue costs a fortune (but you can peek at it).**
The stunning outdoor wedding from Episode 1 wasn't a set‚Äîit‚Äôs the **Aston House at the Grand Walkerhill Seoul**. **3. You need to verify your maps before you go.**
Google Maps is practically useless for walking directions in South Korea due to local regulations. Also, sad news: the "Yongdu-ri Supermarket" was a set built for the show and has been demolished, so don't drive 3 hours just for that specific building!

## PROS AND CONS

### Pros (of the recommended "Seoul + Suwon" itinerary)
1.  **Accessibility**: You can hit the major *chaebol* luxury spots (The Hyundai Seoul, Stone Art Museum) and the romantic garden spots (Irwol Botanic Garden) using just the subway and short taxi rides. No massive travel headaches.
2.  **Instagram Gold**: These locations are visually stunning in real life. The Hyundai Seoul is an architectural marvel, and the Stone Art Museum offers arguably the best panoramic views of Seoul that most tourists miss.
3.  **Food Options**: Unlike the rural locations where food options might be sparse, the Seoul locations are surrounded by world-class dining (like the massive food court at The Hyundai).

### Cons (be honest!)
1.  **The "Fake" Geography**: The show edits locations together deceptively. You can't walk from the Department Store to the Rooftop Garden because they are in two different cities (Seoul and Suwon). Don't expect the geography to match the screen.
2.  **Crowds**: The Hyundai Seoul is legitimately one of the busiest places in Korea. If you go on a weekend, you will be fighting for breathing room, let alone a photo op.
3.  **Missing Sets**: As mentioned, the Yongdu-ri supermarket is gone. If you go to Mungyeong expecting to see Hyun-woo's house, you'll be staring at an empty lot or a generic field.

## STEP-BY-STEP GUIDE

**Guide Title:** The "Queen of Tears" Seoul Weekend Itinerary
**Total Time:** 2 Days
**Difficulty:** Medium

### Step 1: The "Queens" Morning
Start your day at **The Hyundai Seoul** in Yeouido. This is the interior of the Queens Department Store. Head to the upper floors to see the curved white architecture that Hae-in walked through. Grab lunch at the basement food court (it's famous).
> **Pro Tip:** Download the "The Hyundai Seoul" app or check the waiting list kiosks for restaurants immediately when you arrive; wait times can be 2+ hours for popular spots.

### Step 2: The "Queens" Mansion
Take a taxi or bus to Seongbuk-dong to visit the **Korean Stone Art Museum**. This served as the exterior for the Queens family mansion. It‚Äôs quiet, serene, and offers a killer view of the city‚Äîperfect for pretending you own the place.
> **Pro Tip:** It‚Äôs a steep uphill walk from the nearest bus stop. Definitely take a taxi from Hansung University Station if you aren't in hiking shape.

### Step 3: The Proposal Spot (Aqua Planet)
Head towards Ilsan (northwest of Seoul) to **Aqua Planet Ilsan**. This is where Hyun-woo proposed. It's a bit of a trek from central Seoul (about an hour), so plan accordingly.
> **Pro Tip:** Check the feeding times for the main tank; it makes for a much better photo backdrop than an empty tank.

### Step 4: The Rooftop Garden (Suwon)
On Day 2, take the subway down to Suwon to visit **Irwol Botanic Garden**. This is the stunning glasshouse and garden where they hunted for the raccoon. It‚Äôs gorgeous at sunset.
> **Pro Tip:** While in Suwon, grab "Suwon Galbi" (ribs) for dinner‚Äîit's famous in the region, even if the show featured Yongdu-ri pears!

## WARNINGS AND TIPS

### üí° Pro Tip
**The "German" Bridge is in Frankfurt, not Berlin.**
The show mixes German locations. The famous "Love Lock Bridge" (Eiserner Steg) is in Frankfurt. If you book a trip to Berlin expecting to see it, you'll be a 4-hour train ride away. Plan your German leg carefully!

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Warning
**Do NOT go to the Yongdu-ri Supermarket location expecting the set.**
I cannot stress this enough. The building was a temporary set in Mungyeong and was demolished. You can visit the **Mungyeong Eco World** (where the family hung out) and the **Gurang-ri Station** (Rail Bike), but the supermarket is gone. Don't waste your gas!

### üì± No Korean Phone/ARC? Here's What To Do
If you are a tourist without an Alien Registration Card (ARC) or Korean phone number, you face two big hurdles: **Reservations** and **Navigation**.
1.  **Navigation:** Google Maps does not give walking directions in Korea. You **must** download **Naver Map** or **KakaoMap**. They have English interfaces. Set them up *before* you leave the airport WiFi.
2.  **Payments:** You cannot use Apple Pay in many places (though it's improving). Get a **WOWPASS** or **NAMANE** card at the airport. These are prepaid debit cards that work on public transport and in stores/restaurants.
3.  **Taxis:** Download **Uber** (it works in Korea and calls local taxis) or **Kakao T**. For Kakao T, without a Korean card, select "Pay to Driver" and pay with cash or your physical credit card at the end of the ride.

## FAQ

### Q1: Where is the Queens Department Store filmed?
The interior scenes were filmed at **The Hyundai Seoul** in Yeouido, which is famous for its "Sounds Forest" indoor garden. However, the specific rooftop garden scenes where the leads walk were filmed at **Irwol Botanic Garden** in Suwon.

### Q2: Can I visit the wedding venue?
Yes and no. The wedding was filmed at **Aston House**, a private banquet hall at the **Grand Walkerhill Seoul** hotel. You can't just walk onto the lawn if there's a private event (which there usually is on weekends), but you can visit the hotel, explore the public grounds, and see the building from the outside.

### Q3: Where is Yongdu-ri village located?
Yongdu-ri is fictional. The rural scenes were filmed in **Mungyeong**, specifically around **Yongyeon-ri**. The pear blossom fields from the helicopter scene are in **Asan** (Dunpo Sinwang Farm), which is a totally different city.

### Q4: Is the aquarium scene real?
Yes! The proposal and reunion scenes were filmed at **Aqua Planet Ilsan**. It‚Äôs a large aquarium complex that also has a small zoo (The Jungle). It is open to the public daily.

### Q5: How much does it cost to visit the locations?
Most are free or cheap! The Hyundai Seoul is free (unless you shop). Stone Art Museum is ‚Ç©7,000 ($5). Aqua Planet is about ‚Ç©31,000 ($23). Mirim Bunsik is a cheap eatery where a meal is under $10. The only expensive part is the flight to Germany!

## ARTICLE BODY

### Section 1: Answer the Main Question First!
Look, the short answer is that *Queen of Tears* is a masterclass in location scouting, blending the hyper-modern luxury of Seoul with the rustic charm of the Korean countryside‚Äîand a sprinkle of European romance. If you're asking, "Where is the Queens Department Store?", it‚Äôs **The Hyundai Seoul** in Yeouido. You can go there right now, walk the same floors as Hong Hae-in, and marvel at the architecture. Whether you want to drop $100 on a steak where the *chaebols* eat or just spend $2 hitting baseballs like a stressed-out lawyer, you can recreate the entire drama itinerary.

### Section 2: The Background Story
So, why do these locations hit so hard? *Queen of Tears* isn't just a romance; it's a clash of worlds. You have the Queens Group family, representing the absolute peak of Korean wealth (*chaebol* life), and the Yongdu-ri family, representing rural warmth and simplicity. The production team needed locations that screamed "money" and locations that screamed "home."

For the "money" side, they chose **The Hyundai Seoul**, which opened in 2021 and is basically the trendiest, most futuristic mall in Korea right now. It represents Hae-in‚Äôs ambition. For the "home" side, they built a massive open set in **Mungyeong** for the supermarket and house. Sadly, as is common with many K-drama sets built on rented land, the supermarket set was demolished shortly after filming wrapped in 2024. But the surrounding area, including the rail bike tracks and the agricultural backdrop, remains exactly as it was. The German locations‚ÄîBerlin and Potsdam‚Äîwere chosen to represent the couple's honeymoon phase, a time of fairy-tale happiness before the "tears" started.

### Section 3: Breaking Down Your Options
Okay, let‚Äôs be real about your itinerary. Unless you have unlimited time and budget, you probably can‚Äôt visit *every single* spot.
