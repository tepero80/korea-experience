## FAQ

### Q1: Is Korean temple food automatically vegan?

Yes! Authentic Korean temple food (Sachal Eumsik) excludes meat, fish, and the "five pungent vegetables" (garlic, green onions, chives, wild rocambole, and leeks), making it 100% plant-based and low-irritant.

### Q2: Do I have to take my shoes off at these restaurants?

Almost always. Hanok restaurants are built on raised wooden platforms. Wear nice, hole-free socks because you'll likely be leaving your shoes at the entrance in a designated locker.

### Q3: Can I get vegan "Korean Fried Chicken" in a Hanok?

Absolutely. Spots like **Vegan Insa** and **Osegye Hyang** specialize in veganized street food. Their "soy chicken" is incredibly crispy and often served with a sweet and spicy glaze that tastes exactly like the original.

### Q4: Are these restaurants child-friendly?

Yes, Hanoks like **Vegan Insa** are very welcoming to families and have clean, accessible facilities. The open courtyards also provide a bit of breathing room compared to cramped modern cafes.

### Q5: What if I don't like spicy food?

You're in luck. While Korean food is known for heat, temple food is famously mild and focuses on the natural sweetness of vegetables. Just look for dishes like "Yeonbap" (lotus leaf rice) or "Burdock Japchae."
