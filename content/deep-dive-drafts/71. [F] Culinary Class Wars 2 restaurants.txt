## FAQ

### Q1: Can I visit the winner Choi Kang-rok's restaurant?
No, unfortunately not right now. Chef Choi Kang-rok closed his restaurant, *Neo*, in late 2024 (shortly before the finale aired). He stated he wants to avoid the chaos of TV fame affecting his food quality. He is currently focusing on YouTube ("Food Otaku") and his product line with GS25.

### Q2: Is "Cooking Monster" opening a restaurant?
Yes! Chef Lee Ha-sung (aka Cooking Monster) is opening a restaurant called **Oyatte** in New York City, targeting a Spring 2026 launch. He was the runner-up and is taking his momentum to the global stage, so if you're in the US, you might get to taste his food sooner than people in Seoul!

### Q3: Which restaurant is the easiest to get into?
Generally, the larger restaurants or those with multiple branches are easier. **Caden** (by White Spoon Chef Jung Ho-young) has huge capacity and serves casual udon/izakaya food. **Sam Sam Sam** (Trend King) is tough but takes walk-ins if you queue early. Fine dining spots like *Soign√©* are the hardest.

### Q4: Are the convenience store collabs actually good?
Honestly? Yes. Korean convenience store food is high quality to begin with. The *Choi Kang-rok* Sesame Tofu Soup and *Seoul Mom* Bulgogi Kimbap at GS25 are legitimate meals, not just cheap snacks. They were developed with the chefs' direct input.

### Q5: Who is the "Trend King" and where is his place?
"Trend King" is Chef **Kim Hoon**. His restaurant is **Sam Sam Sam** in Yongsan (near the HYBE building). It's famous for its "San Francisco vibes" and the lasagna. It's extremely Instagrammable, which fits his nickname perfectly.
