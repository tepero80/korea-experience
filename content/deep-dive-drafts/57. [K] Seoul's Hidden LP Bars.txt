## FAQ

### Q1: Can I request K-Pop songs at these bars?
**Honestly? Usually no.** While some casual spots might play old-school Korean pop (Gayo), the "Listening Bars" focus on Jazz, Soul, Funk, and Rare Groove. Requesting BTS or Blackpink at a serious audiophile bar like *Kompakt* is a bit of a faux pas. Read the room first!

### Q2: Is there a dress code?
**Not really, but 'Seoul Cool' is a thing.** You won't be rejected for wearing sneakers, but these crowds tend to be stylish. Think minimalist, smart-casual. You'll fit in better with a nice coat and boots than in gym shorts and flip-flops.

### Q3: Are these bars solo-traveler friendly?
**100% Yes.** In fact, they are arguably the *best* places for solo travelers in Seoul. The layout is often designed for solitary listening. Sitting at the bar alone with a book and a whiskey is considered peak sophistication here, not "lonely."

### Q4: How expensive is "expensive"?
**Prepare your wallet.** A Gin & Tonic might cost ‚Ç©18,000 ($13). A cover charge might be ‚Ç©20,000 ($15) which includes one drink. A night out with 3 drinks can easily run you $50-$60 USD per person. You are paying for the atmosphere and the equipment maintenance.

### Q5: Do they serve food?
**It varies.** Places like *Hills & Europa* are famous for their food (specifically the pudding!). Others, like *Electric Shoes*, might only have light bar snacks (cheese plate, dried squid). Don't go to a hardcore listening bar on an empty stomach; eat K-BBQ first!

### Section 2: The Background Story
