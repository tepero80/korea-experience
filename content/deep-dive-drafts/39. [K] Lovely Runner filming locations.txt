## PROS AND CONS

### Pros (of the Suwon Day Trip)
1.  **Walkability is King**: Once you get to Haenggung-dong, you can walk to about 70% of the key filming locations. Sol's house, Sun-jae's house, the mural alleys, and the confession bridge are all within a 15-minute radius. It‚Äôs efficient and saves your feet.
2.  **It‚Äôs Not Just a Set**: This area is a UNESCO World Heritage site (Hwaseong Fortress). So, even if your travel buddy isn't a fan of the show, they‚Äôll still love the traditional architecture, cool cafes, and history. It‚Äôs a win-win.
3.  **The Cafe is Real**: Unlike some dramas where the "house" is just a facade on a soundstage, Sol's house is a functioning cafe called **Mong Ted**. You can actually go in, sit down, and soak up the vibes (and eat their famous salt bread).

### Cons (be honest!)
1.  **Private Residence Awkwardness**: Sun-jae's house (the blue gate right across from Sol's) is a real person's home. You can't go inside, and there are signs asking you to be quiet. It can feel a little invasive if there are too many loud fans around.
2.  **School Access is Tricky**: The high school scenes were filmed at Choongang High School in Seoul. It‚Äôs an active school, so tourists are often banned from entering the grounds during school hours or altogether, meaning you might only get a gate selfie.
3.  **Distance for the "Extras"**: While Suwon covers the main stuff, the amusement park (Sapgyoho) and the beach scenes are far. You really need to dedicate separate days or rent a car to hit those outlying spots without stress.

## STEP-BY-STEP GUIDE

**Guide Title:** The "Im Sol" Suwon Pilgrimage Loop
**Total Time:** 5-6 Hours (leisurely pace)
**Difficulty:** Easy

### Step 1: Get to Suwon and "Mong Ted"
Take the subway (Line 1 or Suin-Bundang Line) to **Suwon Station**. From there, hop on a bus (lots of options like 11, 13, or 35) to the **Hwaseong Haenggung** stop. Walk into the residential alleys of Haenggung-dong. Navigate to **Mong Ted** (Sol's House).
> **Pro Tip:** Go on a weekday morning if you can. On weekends, the line to take a photo in front of the yellow umbrella spot (if it's out) or the blue gate can get long!

### Step 2: The Blue Gate (Sun-jae‚Äôs House)
Literally, just turn around 180 degrees from Mong Ted. The house with the blue gate is right there. It‚Äôs shockingly close‚Äîthe drama made the street look slightly wider, but in reality, they are practically neighbors.
> **Pro Tip:** Remember, people live here! Whisper, snap your pics quickly, and don't lean on their gate or doorbell.

### Step 3: The Confession Bridge (Suwoncheon Stream)
Walk towards the Hwahongmun Gate (the big water gate). Just slightly downstream, you‚Äôll see the stepping stones and the small bridge where the famous confession scene happened. It's especially pretty at sunset.
> **Pro Tip:** In summer, the stream is lush and green; in winter, it looks exactly like the tragic scenes in the drama. Dress accordingly!

### Step 4: Fortress Wall Walk & Yongyeon Pond
Walk up the stairs to the fortress walls. Head toward **Banghwasuryujeong Pavilion** and look down at **Yongyeon Pond**. This is where Sun-jae taught Sol how to ride a bike. It‚Äôs gorgeous at night when the walls are lit up.
> **Pro Tip:** Bring a picnic mat if the weather is nice. This is a huge hangout spot for locals, not just drama fans.

### üì± No Korean Phone/ARC? Here's What To Do
This is CRITICAL. You cannot use apps like Uber or Google Maps effectively here.
1.  **Data is Life:** Get a **USIM or eSIM** (LG U+ or KT are reliable) at Incheon Airport immediately upon arrival. You need a Korean number (usually starts with 010) for some restaurant waiting lists (CatchTable).

## FAQ

### Q1: Is Sol's house actually yellow in real life?
Yes! The building that serves as Sol's house, the Mong Ted cafe, has that warm, yellowish brick/paint aesthetic. However, the specific "video store" props and the exact yellow umbrella stand might not always be set up exactly as they were in the drama, depending on when you visit.

### Q2: Can I enter the high school where they filmed?
It's complicated. The school is **Choongang High School** in Seoul. It is a functioning all-boys school. Generally, they allow tourists to enter the grounds *only* on weekends or holidays, but this policy changes frequently. Do not try to enter during school hours (Mon-Fri)‚Äîsecurity will stop you.

### Q3: How far is the amusement park from Seoul?
Sapgyoho Amusement Park (where they rode the Ferris Wheel) is in Dangjin. It takes about 1.5 to 2 hours by bus from Seoul Nambu Terminal. It's a bit of a trek for just one location, so plan to spend the evening there to see the lights.

### Q4: Is the bridge they cross really in Seoul?
The bridge where Sol tries to stop Sun-jae (and where they meet in winter) is actually **Chuncheon Bridge** in Gangwon-do, not Seoul. It's quite a distance away (about 1.5 - 2 hours from Seoul). The one appearing in the "underworld" vision is Geumgang Bridge in Gongju.

### Q5: Do I need to book a tour to see these places?
Honestly, no. Suwon is incredibly easy to navigate on your own using Naver Map. However, if you want to hit the far-flung spots like the beach or the amusement park in one day without driving, a private K-drama tour van might be worth the investment.

Filming started back in **2023**, capturing the changing seasons that are so crucial to the plot (hello, cherry blossoms and snow!).
