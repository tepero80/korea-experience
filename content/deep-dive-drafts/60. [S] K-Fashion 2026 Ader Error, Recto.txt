## FAQ

### Q1: Are Ader Error and Recto cheaper in Korea than on Ssense/Farfetch?
**Yes, absolutely.** Between the lack of import duties, the lower retail price (MSRP is usually 20-30% lower domestically), and the ~7% tax refund you get back, you can save hundreds of dollars on a single coat compared to buying it in the US or Europe.

### Q2: Do I really need to wait in line?
**In 2026? Yes.** The hype hasn't died; it's just evolved. Weekends in Seongsu are a zoo. However, if you go to a department store location (like Shinsegae Gangnam or Centum City in Busan), there is almost never a line. You trade the "cool interior" for convenience.

### Q3: What is the "Immediate Tax Refund" change for 2026?
Previously, the limit for getting cash back *instantly* at the register was lower (500k KRW). Now, you can buy items up to **1 million KRW** (about $750 USD) and get the tax deducted right there. The total cap for your whole trip is **5 million KRW**. This is huge for buying luxury coats at Recto.

### Q4: Can I try on clothes at Ader Error?
**Yes, but...** usually only 1-2 items at a time, and you must wear a "face cover" (a weird white hood they give you) to prevent makeup transfer. They are militant about this.

### Q5: Is Recto strictly minimal?
It used to be, but the 2025/2026 collections have introduced more texture‚Äîhairy wools, curved heels, and interesting distinct silhouettes. It's "minimal with a twist." It's not boring basics; it's "I'm the main character in a lawyer drama" energy.
