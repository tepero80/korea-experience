## Must-Visit Cherry Blossom Destinations

### Jinhae Gunhangje: The Sovereign Festival

The **Jinhae Cherry Blossom Festival (Gunhangje)** remains the crown jewel of Korea's spring celebrations, featuring over **350,000 cherry trees**. In 2026, the festival runs from March 23 to April 12.

Cherry blossoms are just the start. Discover other [spring festivals in Korea beyond cherry blossoms](/blog/spring-in-korea-cherry-blossoms-and-festivals).

**Yeojwacheon Stream**: Known for the "Romance Bridge," this site features a 1.5-kilometer canal where cherry trees form a continuous tunnel. Enhanced 2026 night lighting creates a mirrored effect of pink blossoms against the water.

**Gyeonghwa Station**: This decommissioned station offers the quintessential "floral rain" experience. Visit before April 5 to witness the full canopy over the railway tracks.

### Seoul: Urban Integration and Palace Aesthetics

**Yeouido Spring Flower Festival**: Spanning 1.7 kilometers along the Han River with 1,886 cherry trees. The 2026 peak is projected for March 28 to April 3. The "Yunjung-ro" road becomes pedestrian-only during peak.

**Seokchon Lake**: Encircling the lake in Songpa-gu, best viewed with the Lotte World Tower backdrop. 2026 features "Piano on the Lake" performances and cherry blossom cinema. Peak: March 30 to April 5.

**Gyeongbokgung Palace**: The blossoms over Gyeonghoeru Pavilion are particularly photogenic. Wearing a Hanbok grants free entryâ€”expect record participation in 2026.

### Jeju: The King Cherry Heritage

Jeju's 2026 season is defined by the **wangbeot-namu (King Cherry)**. These trees are native only to the southern slopes of Hallasan and are prized for their fuller, deeper pink petals.

**Dual-Bloom Phenomenon**: In mid-April, visitors can witness a rare "dual bloom" as fading pink cherry blossoms overlap with vibrant yellow canola (yu-chae-kkot) fields.
