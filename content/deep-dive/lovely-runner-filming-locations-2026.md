## The Truth About the Locations: Suwon vs. Seoul

So, you want to know where *Lovely Runner* was filmed so you can breathe the same air as Sun-jae and Sol? **Here is the deal: You need to go to Suwon, not Seoul.**

While there are a few spots scattered around the capital‚Äîwhich we cover in our guide to [K-Drama filming locations in Seoul 2026](/blog/k-drama-filming-locations-in-seoul-2026)‚Äîthe beating heart of the drama is located in the **Haenggung-dong neighborhood of Suwon**, about an hour south of Seoul. This includes Sol‚Äôs house, Sun-jae‚Äôs blue-gated house, and those romantic alleyways.

Specifically, you are looking for a cafe called **Mong Ted** (Sol's house). The best part? It is completely accessible. You do not need special permission or a tour guide. You just need a subway card with about **‚Ç©3,000** on it and a pair of comfortable walking shoes. Standing in that alleyway feels surreal, like Sun-jae is about to walk out with an umbrella any second.

### Why Suwon Was Chosen

Okay, let's rewind a bit. Why Suwon? If you have watched the show (and if you haven't, stop reading and go watch it!), you know the vibe is very nostalgic, warm, and intimate. Seoul is amazing, but it is often too modern and frantic to capture that specific "first love in a quiet neighborhood" feeling.

The production team chose Haenggung-dong because strict development restrictions protect it‚Äîit sits right next to the UNESCO World Heritage Hwaseong Fortress. This means no skyscrapers, lots of preserved traditional roofs, and a maze of cozy alleys that look exactly like they did 15 years ago. It was the perfect canvas for a time-slip drama.

Filming started back in **2023**, capturing the changing seasons that are so crucial to the plot (hello, cherry blossoms and snow!). By the time the show aired in April 2024, locals started noticing lines forming in front of a certain cafe. Fast forward to 2026, and this quiet neighborhood has become a full-blown pilgrimage site for fans from all over the world. It is not just a set; it is a real, breathing community that now holds a special place in K-drama history.

## Planning Your Pilgrimage Strategy

Now, you have a few choices on how to tackle this pilgrimage, depending on how obsessed you are.

**Option A: The "Casual Fan" (Seoul Only)**
If you are only in Korea for a few days and cannot leave Seoul, you can still catch a vibe. You can visit **Choongang High School** near Anguk Station. This is the school they attended. The architecture is stunning‚Äîvery gothic and moody. Just remember, you can usually only peek through the gates on weekdays. You can also eat at **Soegojip** in Insadong (Sun-jae's dad's restaurant) or hit **DDP** (Dongdaemun Design Plaza) where the concert scenes were filmed.

**Option B: The "Im Sol" Experience (Suwon Day Trip)**
This is what I recommend for 90% of you. Take the subway to Suwon. Spend the afternoon walking from Mong Ted to the Confession Bridge. It is walkable, stress-free, and hits all the emotional high notes. You can do this easily in half a day.

**Option C: The "Die-Hard" (Road Trip)**
If you want to see *everything*, including the Ferris Wheel at **Sapgyoho** and the reservoir at **Dongtan Lake Park**, you are looking at a multi-day trip or a rental car situation. These spots are far apart. Sapgyoho is in Dangjin, which is a solid 1.5 to 2 hours away from Seoul by bus. It is doable, but it is a commitment.

## The Complete How-To (Suwon Edition)

Let's walk through the Suwon loop because that is the money shot.

First, download **Naver Map**. Type in "Suwon Station." From Seoul Station, take **Line 1** (the dark blue line) all the way to Suwon. It takes about an hour. Once you exit Suwon Station, head to the bus transfer center. You are looking for buses heading to **Hwaseong Haenggung** (Palace). Buses 11, 13, or even the 35 will get you there.

Once you get off at the palace, cross the street and dive into the alleys. Put "Mong Ted" into your map. As you walk, you will start recognizing the murals. When you turn the corner and see the yellow brick building, prepare to gasp. It really looks just like Sol's house. Go inside! Order an **Iced Americano** and their **Salt Bread** (it is famous for a reason‚Äîcrispy outside, fluffy inside). Sit by the window and look out. Directly across the narrow street is the **Blue Gate**. That is Sun-jae‚Äôs house.

After you have taken 500 photos, walk towards the stream (Suwoncheon). Follow the water north until you see the big stone floodgate (Hwahongmun). Just before it, there is a small stepping-stone bridge. That is where the confession happened. It is incredibly peaceful. If you have energy left, climb the hill to the **Banghwasuryujeong Pavilion**. It is the perfect place to watch the sunset and pretend you are waiting for a time slip.

## Essential Etiquette and Tips

Here are the pro tips that will save you from embarrassment and help you navigate [unwritten social rules in South Korea](/blog/korea-social-rules-local-guide-2026).

First, **Sun-jae‚Äôs house is a private home.** I cannot stress this enough. There are real people living there. They are generally nice to fans, but please do not shout "SUN-JAE YAAAA!" at the top of your lungs at 8 AM. Be cool. Take your pic and move on.

Second, **Mong Ted is closed on Wednesdays.** Imagine traveling all the way there and finding the gate locked. That would be tragic. Check their Instagram (@__mongted) before you go just in case of sudden closures.

Third, the **"Video Rental Shop"** sign is not there anymore. The production crew put that up for filming. The cafe has its own signage. Do not be confused if it looks slightly cleaner and more modern than in the drama.

**CRITICAL TIP regarding Transport:** If you do not have a Korean ARC (Alien Registration Card), you cannot just sign up for any Korean app. Make sure you have **Kakao T** installed for taxis, but set payment to "Pay to Driver." If you get stranded in Suwon late at night, hailing a taxi on the street can be tough in 2026. Having the app is a lifesaver. Also, get the **Climate Card** (unlimited transit card) if you are staying in Seoul for a while‚Äîit covers the subway ride to Suwon!
